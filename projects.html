Sneak Peek

During my spare time, I worked on a personal project focused on horror/puzzle genre which was a new experience for me. I used Unity 3D to put my skills to the test, to develop the game. Set in a spooky atmosphere, players must navigate with a flashlight to search for keys to unlock a door to escape while avoiding a lurking monster. The monster emits growling sounds as it moves, which players can use to their advantage to avoid being caught. This project allowed me to experiment with Unity’s tools and enhance my scripting skills like object movement, scene management and game mechanics. I learned how creating the appropriate atmosphere and sound can enhance the tension and paranoia in my gameplay.

TTSH Nurses Appreciation Day 2022

For my final year project at NYP, my team and I developed a nostalgic game, bringing in familiar Singaporean games to nurses at Tan Tock Seng Hospital (TTSH) during Nurses Appreciation Day 2022. We developed 3 mini games: Scissors Paper Stone, Five Stones and Chapteh. I was tasked to create the Chapteh game where the player uses the mouse to kick the chapteh into different coloured hoops to earn points. The gameplay mechanic allowed the player to control the power of their kicks. A powerful kick allows the chapteh to fly higher, which the player stands a better chance to gain more points. Not only did this project help to sharpen my programming skills, but also offering a fun time for the nursing staff at their well-deserved break.
